Teaching

I have been a Graduate Student Instructor (GSI) for Berkeley Sociology's two-semester graduate statistics course and for two undergraduate classes (“Social Inequality” and “Research Methods”). If you are interested in content from those, please contact me.

I have been a consultant and workshop instructor for UC Berkeley’s D-Lab, a center for data intensive social science. Go here for the files of my January 2015 Stata Intensive.

Relatedly, these are some useful Stata resources

  • a good intro tutorial from German Rodriguez
  • the UCLA Statistical Consulting Group
  • the Stata forum
  • some user-written Stata programs
    • estout (make great tables)
    • coefplot [PDF] (visualize your results!)
    • binscatter (show binned scatterplots)
    • grc1leg (combine multiple graphs with one legend)

I have also been a Graduate student mentor for the Cal-ADAR program (Advancing Diversity in Aging Research), a program to gives underrepresented students undergraduate research experience in demography. For more information, visit the program's website.
