I have been a Graduate Student Instructor (GSI) for Berkeley Sociology's two-semester graduate quantitative methods course and for two undergraduate classes (“Social Inequality” and “Research Methods”). If you are interested in content from those, please contact me.

I have been a mentor for the Cal-ADAR program (Advancing Diversity in Aging Research), a program supported by the National Institutes of Aging to give underrepresented students undergraduate research experience in demography. For more information or to apply, visit the program's website.

I have been a consultant and workshop instructor for UC Berkeley’s D-Lab, a center for data intensive social science. Here are files from my one of my Stata Intensives.

  • the UCLA Statistical Consulting Group
  • the Stata forum

    • coefplot [PDF] (visualize your results!)

    • grc1leg (combine multiple graphs with one legend)
    • drarea (overlapping range plots)
