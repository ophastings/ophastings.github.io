Teaching

Go here for the files of my January 2015 Stata Intensive. I have taught several workshops for D-Lab on data management, statistics, and graphics in Stata. Previously I was also a data and statistical consultant for D-Lab.

I have also been the Graduate Student Instructor for Berkeley Sociology's two-semester graduate statistics course (2012-2013) and one-semester undergraduate methods course (2011). If you are interested in content from those, please contact me.

Tools

 I use Stata a great deal for my work. I have found these resources especially useful:
  • a good intro tutorial from German Rodriguez
  • the UCLA Statistical Consulting Group
  • the Stata forum
  • some user-written Stata programs
    • estout (for great tables)
    • binscatter (for binned scatterplots)
    • grc1leg (for combining multiple graphs with one legend)

In addition to Stata, I enjoy working with LaTeX, Evernote, Omnifocus, a Macbook Pro, and Philz Coffee.
